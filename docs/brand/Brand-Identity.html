# Brand Identity Document Compiled by Draper — 23/06/2025 **How to use this document** This is Levera's brand guide. It is written for anyone who makes something in Levera's name: copy, UI, marketing, social, or code. Use the relevant section whenever you make something. If you are an LLM, treat every section as a hard constraint, not a suggestion. --- ## 1. Name **Levera** — from the Latin "levare": to lift, to raise, to elevate. A lever is a simple tool that turns small effort into large result. That is the product in one word. The name works for children and adults equally. It carries no negative meaning in Russian, English, or the major European languages. It is short, readable, and leaves room for the brand to fill it with meaning. --- ## 2. Purpose Путь к мечте не бывает прямым. Человек падает, забывает цели, жизнь бьёт по планам. Levera — это система, которая видит наклонности твои и твоего ребёнка, помнит, куда вы идёте, и делает путь к результату интересным. Даже после падения — мы помогаем встать и дойти до жизни, о которой мечтаешь. **How to use this purpose:** This is the internal compass. Every product decision, every feature, every marketing choice should be tested against it. If a feature adds noise rather than clarity, it contradicts the purpose. If a campaign creates pressure rather than interest, it contradicts the purpose. The purpose is written in Russian because that is where the founder's voice lives — translate it when needed, but do not dilute it. --- ## 3. Positioning **"Твой путь. Твой темп. Твой результат."** ("Your path. Your pace. Your result.") Three short sentences, each beginning with "твой" (yours). This is deliberate. The positioning puts ownership with the user — not the app, not the parent, not the system. Levera is a tool, not a teacher. The repetition creates rhythm and makes the line easy to remember and easy to use. Use this line on the website hero, App Store description, pitch decks, and any marketing surface where a single line must do the work. Do not explain it. Let it stand alone. --- ## 4. Brand Values Brand values are the three principles that guide every decision Levera makes — what to build, what to say, what to refuse. When a choice is unclear, return to these. ### Value 1 — Честный прогресс (Honest Progress) Levera believes that progress which cannot be measured is not progress. The app shows the real picture — where you are growing, where you are stuck, where a skill is fading. It does not hide uncomfortable data. It does not congratulate you for opening the app. Honesty is the foundation of trust. | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't | |---|---| | Show skill gaps as clearly as achievements | Hide or soften uncomfortable data | | Make specific, verifiable promises | Use vague words like "development" without evidence | | Speak directly, but with respect | Praise effort alone when there is no result | ### Value 2 — Заслуженное остаётся (Earned Stays) Cheap dopamine is everywhere. Levera is built on the opposite principle: reward comes after real effort, and that is exactly why it means something. A skill built through spaced repetition and graduated challenge does not fade. What is truly earned stays in the person — not just in the app. | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't | |---|---| | Connect rewards to real, measurable achievement | Give rewards for simply opening the app | | Think about the long-term effect of every mechanic | Optimise for the session at the expense of the habit | | Let the user feel genuine authorship over their result | Build dependency where a habit would serve better | ### Value 3 — Вместе быстрее (Together is Faster) The best results do not happen alone. Family, mentor, community — these are not additions to the system, they are part of it. A child who completes a quest and reviews it with their parent on Sunday evening is building something bigger than a skill. A parent who sees real progress feels pride, not anxiety. Synergy multiplies every individual effort. | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't | |---|---| | Design for shared use — parent and child, or peer groups | Make learning a solitary, isolated process | | Support the parent as an active participant, not a spectator | Ignore the user's social and family context | | Show family or group progress, not only individual stats | Replace human connection with app mechanics | --- ## 5. Tone of Voice Levera speaks like a person, not like an app. There is a real human behind every line — someone who has spent three years watching children learn, who knows what it feels like to fall off a habit and fight to come back, who has read the science and lived the practice. The voice is warm, direct, and never performs enthusiasm it does not feel. It does not say "Amazing work, champion!" It says "Three weeks ago you could not do this. Now you can." It does not explain what the user already knows. It does not create anxiety. It speaks to people as capable adults and curious children — not as customers to be retained. Think: a coach who respects you enough to tell the truth. Not a cheerleader. Not a bureaucrat. ### Tone Do's & Don'ts | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't | |---|---| | Speak as if you know this person | Use exclamation marks to perform energy you do not have | | Acknowledge difficulty without drama | Pretend everything is always easy | | Use short, plain words | Hide behind terms or jargon | ### Tone Examples | ❌ Don't write this | ✅ Write this instead | |---|---| | "Поздравляем с достижением нового уровня компетентности! 🏆🎉" | "Три недели назад ты не умел этого. Теперь умеешь." | | "Систематическое выполнение заданий обеспечит оптимальный результат!" | "Скучно? Значит, задача слишком лёгкая. Поднимем уровень." | | "Вы не выполнили задание. Ваш прогресс под угрозой." | "Выбился из ритма — бывает. С чего начнём сегодня?" | --- ## 6. Counter-positioning The current market is split between chore apps with coin rewards (Greenlight, BusyKid, Kikaroo, TaskStars) and AI task generators (FamilyHero, KidTasks AI). All of them share one logic: task → reward → done. They measure activity, not growth. They were built for parents who want to delegate household management, not for people who want to build real, lasting skills. Levera takes a fundamentally different position on all three of their core assumptions. ### The Counter-Positions Levera Owns **Position 1 — Не дофамин, а достижение (Not dopamine — achievement)** "Дешёвый дофамин везде. Мы строим систему, где награда приходит после настоящего усилия. Именно поэтому она имеет вес — и остаётся." The market is optimised for engagement. Levera is optimised for results. The distinction matters most when a user opens the app six months later and still remembers what they learned. **Position 2 — Не идеальный план, а живая система (Not a perfect plan — a living system)** "Жизнь сбивает с пути — болезни, усталость, форс-мажор. Мы строим привычку, которая знает, что путь не бывает прямым — и помогает пройти его, получая результат и удовольствие от самого пути." Other apps break when the user does. Levera is designed for real life, not the ideal version of it. The return mechanic is a feature, not a failure state. **Position 3 — Не инструмент контроля, а инструмент доверия (Not a control tool — a trust tool)** "Большинство приложений ставят родителя над ребёнком. Мы ставим их рядом — общий прогресс, общий ритуал, общий результат." Control creates resistance. Trust creates habits. The weekly family review ritual is not a reporting tool — it is a moment of shared pride. ### In Practice Use these positions in copy when drawing a contrast with the status quo — not by naming competitors directly, but by naming the problem they represent. "Most apps pay children to do tasks. We teach them to want to." Do not attack by name. Let the contrast speak. --- ## 7. Golden Circle The Golden Circle (Simon Sinek) is the primary source of copy across all contexts — website, onboarding, ads, pitch decks. When you need to write about Levera, start here. The Why drives the emotional hook, the How explains the approach, the What describes the product plainly. ### Why Most people learn — and forget. They spend time, money, and energy without seeing real results. We believe every person deserves to see their growth honestly — and to have a system that works even when life does not go to plan. The system should carry the weight of memory and planning, so the person can carry the weight of doing. ### How We connect proven learning science — Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development, Ebbinghaus spaced repetition, Self-Determination Theory — with a living system that adapts to each person's pace and naклонности. Not a perfect plan. A route that knows how to recover. The AI does the remembering. The person does the growing. ### What An app that helps children and adults build real skills through tasks calibrated to their exact level — and see genuine progress together with their family. For children: quests, a skill tree, and a wish-list mechanic. For adults: a personal development system that runs quietly in the background and shows up when it matters. --- ## 8. Brand Inspirations The brands whose DNA Levera draws from. Each entry explains what to take and where to apply it. ### Apple **What it is:** The world's most recognised consumer technology company, known for hardware, software, and a design philosophy that removes everything that is not essential. **What to take:** Apple's principle that good design is not about adding — it is about removing. Every screen, every interaction, every word in Levera should pass the test: does this earn its place? The parent should be able to set up a task in seconds. The child should know exactly what to do next without reading instructions. **Where to apply it:** UX and product design — every screen and interaction. Onboarding flow. Copy length (shorter is always better). **Search stem:** Apple minimal UI ### Duolingo **What it is:** A language learning app that became the world's most downloaded education product by making progress visible, habitual, and emotionally engaging. **What to take:** Duolingo's core insight: progress is more motivating than reward. The streak mechanic, the XP bar, the skill tree — these are not games, they are mirrors. Levera takes the visual language of progress (coloured skill indicators, clear level states, satisfying completion moments) but applies it without the anxiety mechanics. No punishment for missing a day. Progress that feels earned, not extracted. **Where to apply it:** Child interface design — skill tree, XP system, coloured skill indicators by category (physical, mental, emotional). Progress visualisation throughout. **Search stem:** Duolingo skill progress ### Telegram / Pavel Durov **What it is:** A messaging platform built by a Russian founder on principles of speed, freedom, and refusal to compromise on core values — even under pressure. **What to take:** Durov's approach to product communication: say less, mean more. Telegram's product announcements are plain, confident, and free of marketing language. Levera's communication should carry the same quality — no hype, no over-promise, no performance. Also: the founder's identity as a Russian builder with global ambition is a direct model for Levera's positioning in the Russian/CIS market with an eye toward international scale. **Where to apply it:** All written communication — website, social, PR. Tone of voice across all surfaces. Brand confidence. **Search stem:** Telegram product communication --- ## 9. Aesthetic Inspirations For mood board generation, design briefs, and visual direction. Each reference is written with enough specificity to be passed directly to a visual tool, image generation prompt, or designer. ### Ancient Greek Philosophy Schools (Architecture and Visual Culture — Classical Athens, 5th–4th century BC) **The quality:** Clean geometric forms, white marble surfaces, rational proportions, open colonnaded spaces filled with light and air. The visual world of the Athenian agora and the Academy — where Plato and Aristotle taught — is one of clarity, permanence, and the elevation of the human mind. There is no decoration for decoration's sake. Every element serves a structural or conceptual purpose. The palette is stone white, warm sand, pale gold, and deep shadow. **Extract for:** Overall visual language — UI backgrounds, spacing philosophy, hero imagery. The sense of "ancient intelligence made modern." Typography choices (clean, geometric serifs or modern sans-serifs with classical proportions). **Search stem:** Ancient Greek minimal architecture ### Минималистичный прогрессивный дизайн — "Древнегреческий хайтек" (Minimalist Progressive — "Ancient Greek High-Tech") **The quality:** The aesthetic the founder named: a future that remembers its past. Think clean, light interfaces with subtle references to classical structure — columnar grids, golden ratio spacing, a restrained palette of off-white, warm stone, and a single accent colour that reads as modern (electric blue, deep teal, or oxidised copper). Not retro. Not futurist. The feeling of standing in a restored ancient building that has been fitted with modern glass and light. **Extract for:** App UI visual direction. Hero imagery on the website. Brand colour palette and spacing system. Icon design. **Search stem:** Neo-classical minimal tech UI ### Кодекс чести русского офицера — Царская Россия (Russian Imperial Officer Aesthetic — Late 19th / Early 20th century) **The quality:** Dignified, structured, and quietly proud. The visual world of the Russian Imperial officer — dark navy and gold, precise typographic hierarchy, crest-based insignia, understated decoration on a formal ground. There is no loudness here. Authority is communicated through restraint, not volume. Think the colour of a well-worn leather journal, the weight of a gold seal on a white page, the stillness of a formal portrait. **Extract for:** Brand photography mood — formal but human. Marketing materials for the parent audience (trust, credibility, heritage). Colour accents: deep navy, warm gold, parchment white. Badge and achievement design within the app. **Search stem:** Russian Imperial officer uniform aesthetic
